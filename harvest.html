Subdivide the field into a grid so that we could plan an on-farm experiment to compare two different agronomic practices

In order to conduct a comprehensive on-farm experiment and effectively compare two different agronomic practices, we were given access to yield monitor data collected from previous harvest years for crops such as corn, soybeans, and wheat. The data was meticulously collected by continuously sampling the grain every second as it passed through the combine. Each sample was then tagged with a GPS coordinate to precisely pinpoint its location in the field, and the estimated yield was measured in bushels per acre.

We received multiple datasets representing various harvests from the same field. To simplify our analysis, we trimmed the data to a uniform square of 600 by 400 meters and converted the original GPS coordinates to metric units.

Our primary objective is to partition the field into a grid system to effectively plan and execute our on-farm experiment while comparing the two agronomic practices. By achieving this objective, we aim to gain valuable insights into the effects of different agronomic practices on crop yields and optimize future crop management strategies.
